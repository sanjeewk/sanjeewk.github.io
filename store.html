About Me

I am a second-year undergraduate student from Sri Lanka studying Computer Engineering at The University of Hong Kong, and I like making stuff!

Having, always had a deep interest in tech and solving problems, I started learning to code at the age of 15. Beginning with Arduino programming, I quickly moved on to other programming languages. Upon entry to HKU I was able to make the use of the resources made available to me. In my first year I, along with my team, built and programmed a Rube Goldberg machine and was placed first in the competition.

In my second year I was given the opportunity to travel to Thailand to teach kids the basics of robotics as well as other more complex topics. I was also appointed as a research assistant under Dr. Joe Yuen, where I was in charge of the full stack development of an educational chatbot that assists in teaching students how to code.
